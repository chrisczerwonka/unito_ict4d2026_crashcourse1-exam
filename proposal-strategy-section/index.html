PROPOSAL STRATEGY

A data-driven response to the challenge of education disruption in conflict zones.

Submitted in response to the Education Bridge Initiative (EBI) RFP: Strengthening Education Continuity in Conflict-Affected Regions.

01. CONTEXT

Humanitarian teams in the Sahel possess deep, localized knowledge of the security challenges facing education. However, as conflict events accelerate and displacement patterns shift, this informal knowledge often remains fragmented. There is a critical gap: the lack of a systematic, data-driven monitoring layer that can objectively flag infrastructure at risk across vast and insecure territories like the Burkina Faso-Mali-Niger border regions.

Our project aims to bridge this gap. We don't replace field expertise; we empower it by transforming raw conflict data and school registries into comparable, actionable risk metrics.

02. OBJECTIVES

  1. Develop a Proximity-Based Monitoring Strategy: Establish a rigorous data pipeline that identifies schools within high-risk zones (5km) based on real-time conflict event monitoring.
  2. Create Actionable Visual Tools: Build an interactive portal that allows EBI staff to visualize the "risk landscape" and prioritize schools for emergency support.
  3. Ensure Operational Sustainability: Provide the methodology and automation scripts necessary for EBI to maintain risk mapping independently.

03. APPROACH

Strategic Rationale

We focus on proximity monitoring as our core capability. It is the highest-impact, lowest-cost intervention for data-driven prioritization. Identifying schools near conflict clusters provides an immediate "red flag" system that can trigger field assessments before a school is forced to close.

Methodology

Our approach overlays the ACLED (Conflict) database with HOT-OSM/UNICEF (Infrastructure) locations. We use a geospatial "nearest-neighbor" analysis to classify every school facility based on its distance to the most recent violent event.

AI-Enhanced Efficiency

We leverage AI (Gemini CLI) to automate the technical "heavy lifting." This includes cleaning diverse datasets from HDX and ACLED and generating complex geospatial calculations (Haversine formula), reducing the technical barrier for EBI staff.

04. PROPOSED ACTIVITIES

  • Pilot Risk Mapping: Map 17,000+ school facilities across Burkina Faso, Mali, and Niger, identifying "Critical Risk" facilities (within 5km of conflict).
  • Interactive Dashboard Deployment: Launch a web-based dashboard tracking conflict trends and school vulnerability for regional coordination.
  • Methodology Handover: Document the data pipeline ensuring EBI technical staff can refresh data monthly without external assistance.

05. DELIVERABLES

Deliverable Description
Risk-Classified GeoJSONs Standardized database of schools tagged with risk status and conflict proximity.
Interactive Risk Portal Mobile-responsive web map and analytics dashboard for operational use.
Automated Data Pipeline Suite of Python scripts for data filtering, cleaning, and risk calculation.
Methodology Guide Step-by-step guide detailing data sources, calculation logic, and update procedures.
