# Research Question and RFP Response Angle

**Project:** Strengthening Education Continuity in Conflict-Affected Regions
**Issued by:** Education Bridge Initiative (EBI)
**Course:** ICT for Civic Data – UNITO ICT4D Crash Course
**Date:** April-May 2026

---

## 1. Data Pipeline Step 1: Define

### Geographic Focus
Conflict-affected WAEMU/UEMOA countries, particularly:
* Burkina Faso
* Mali
* Niger
* Northern Benin border regions (affected by spillover insecurity and displacement)

### Hazard Type
* Armed conflict and insecurity
* Forced displacement
* School closures
* Disruption of safe access to education due to regional instability

### Population and Infrastructure Gaps
* Underserved rural and peri-urban communities
* Internally displaced populations (IDPs)
* Children in insecure border regions
* Schools affected by closures, occupation, destruction, or teacher shortages
* Weak educational continuity infrastructure in rapidly shifting environments

### Proposal Argument (Summary)
Use open geospatial data, conflict-event monitoring, population analysis, and AI-assisted mapping tools to identify education access gaps, assess school vulnerability and displacement pressures, and support rapid, data-driven prioritization of education continuity interventions across the WAEMU/UEMOA regions.

---

## 2. Angle: Education Continuity Monitoring as the Backbone of Resilience

### Core Argument
A regional, multi-layered education continuity monitoring and vulnerability intelligence system is the highest-impact investment because it enables **prevention, protection, and response** simultaneously. This transforms fragmented reactions into a coordinated, data-driven education resilience system.

### Why This is a High-Leverage Intervention
Conflict in the Sahel creates systemic disruptions to education access and long-term social stability. Currently, the challenge is a lack of **timely, localized, and operationally actionable information** to help EBI and local authorities prioritize interventions.

#### Affected Areas and Groups:
* Rural and peri-urban schools with weak infrastructure
* Internally displaced children and host communities
* Girls and vulnerable youth at heightened risk of exclusion
* Teacher deployment and retention systems
* Community trust and long-term economic resilience
* Cross-border population movement

---

## 3. How Monitoring Unlocks the Three Pillars

### Pillar 1: Prevention – Risk-Aware Planning
* **Identification:** Pinpoint schools near escalating insecurity hotspots and regions where displacement pressures may overwhelm infrastructure.
* **Analysis:** Monitor areas where child population density increases despite declining school access.
* **Support:** Facilitates proactive contingency planning, safer school placement, and better resource allocation.

### Pillar 2: Protection – Optimizing Allocation
* **Targeting:** Enable support for schools serving displaced populations and prioritize temporary learning spaces.
* **Gaps:** Identify infrastructure and staffing shortages in high-risk zones.
* **Triggers:** Support anticipatory education response triggers tied to conflict escalation indicators.

### Pillar 3: Response – Rapid Intervention
* **Real-time Monitoring:** Faster identification of school closures or access disruptions.
* **Targeting:** Dynamic targeting of emergency education support and tracking displacement-related needs.
* **Deployment:** Coordinate temporary learning infrastructure and mobile/community-based education.

---

## 4. Implementation and Scalability

### Modular Approach
The system is deployable incrementally rather than requiring a large centralized platform immediately.
* **Pilot:** Start in selected high-risk districts across Burkina Faso, Niger, Mali, and northern Benin.
* **Tools:** Use open conflict-event datasets, open school-location data, population/displacement estimates, and lightweight geospatial tools.

### Future Capabilities
* AI-assisted school vulnerability scoring.
* Displacement trend overlays.
* Accessibility and route-risk analysis.
* Early-warning indicators for education disruption.

### Potential Partners
* Ministries of Education
* Regional humanitarian coordination actors
* Telecom operators
* UNICEF and Education Cluster partners
* Local NGOs and community networks

---

## 5. Bottom Line
Monitoring is a **force multiplier** for education resilience. It ensures interventions are timely, targeted, coordinated, and adaptable. It transforms education response from reactive crisis management into a predictive and operationally informed resilience system.
